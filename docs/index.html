— Why this exists

Wispr Flow is great. $15/month for dictation isn't.

Voice-to-text is mostly a thin wrapper over a model someone else trained. That shouldn't be a subscription — it should be a small app you own.

— Open source

Read every line. Fork it. Make it yours.

The whole app is on GitHub, MIT licensed. Because this isn't something you should have to pay for, and because trust is verifiable, not promised.

— Two ways to run it

Bring your Groq key, or stay fully offline.

Paste a free Groq API key for fast, accurate cloud transcription on your own account. Skip the key and it falls back to a model that runs entirely on your Mac.

Either way, no unmute account, no unmute server, no middleman.

— Local-first

Your voice stays yours.

Audio is sent only to Groq via your own key — or never leaves your device. Your key is encrypted in the macOS Keychain. No telemetry, no analytics, nothing phones home.

— How it feels

Tap. Speak. It's there.

Tap Fn anywhere to dictate. Tap ⇪ to give a voice instruction on text you've selected — “make this formal,” “turn into bullets,” “translate to Hindi.”

Sub-second on your existing cursor. No window switching. No copying back. Raw by default, never auto-formatted unless you ask.
